Spread 2 — The Prince Who Looked Down

✨ ☁️
Way up above the clouds, higher than the highest mountain,
there lived a prince named Hwanung.

Every morning, Hwanung walked to
the edge of heaven and looked down.
구름보다 높은 곳에
환웅이라는 왕자가 살고 있었어요.

매일 아침, 환웅은 하늘 끝에 서서
아래를 내려다보았어요.
2 — 3

Spread 3 — What He Saw

🌿 🏔️
He saw rivers that twisted like silver ribbons.
He saw mountains with trees so green
they looked like they were breathing.

"I want to help them,"
Hwanung whispered.
은빛 리본처럼 구불구불한 강이 보였어요.
숨을 쉬는 것처럼 푸른 나무가 가득한 산.

"저 사람들을 돕고 싶어요,"
환웅이 속삭였어요.
4 — 5

Spread 4 — Three Thousand Friends

💫 🌧️
His father smiled. "Then go," he said.

And he gave Hwanung three thousand helpers
and three magical gifts:
the power of wind, the power of rain,
and the power of clouds.

Hwanung flew down to the tallest mountain —
a mountain called Baekdu.
"그럼 가거라," 왕이 말했어요.

바람의 힘, 비의 힘, 구름의 힘.

환웅은 백두산으로 날아갔어요.
6 — 7

Spread 5 — Two Friends on a Mountain

🐾 🌸
Now, on that mountain lived two friends.

A tiger named Ho-rang,
who was loud and fast
and never, ever sat still.

And a bear named Gom-i,
who was quiet and warm
and could wait for anything.
그 산에는 두 친구가 살고 있었어요.

시끄럽고 빠른 호랑이, 호랑.

조용하고 따뜻한 곰, 곰이.
8 — 9

Spread 6 — The Wish

🌳
"We want to become human," they said.
"We want to walk in the villages
and eat warm rice
and sing songs around the fire."

Hwanung looked at them for a long time.

"I can help you.
But it will be very, very hard."
"사람이 되고 싶어요."

"도와줄 수 있어.
하지만 아주, 아주 힘들 거야."
10 — 11

Spread 7 — The Test

🕯️
"Go into the dark cave," he said.
"Eat only this. See no sunlight.
If you can last one hundred days…
you will become human."

Ho-rang grabbed the garlic.
"Easy!" he said.

Gom-i said nothing.
She just walked into the darkness.
"백 일을 견딜 수 있다면…
사람이 될 것이다."

"쉽지!" 호랑이 말했어요.

곰이는 어둠 속으로 걸어갔어요.
12 — 13

Spread 8 — Ho-rang Gives Up

☀️
Day one: "This garlic is TERRIBLE."

Day ten: "I MISS the sun."

Day twenty: "I CAN'T DO THIS!"
He burst out of the cave
and ran into the bright, bright sunlight.

Gom-i watched him go.
But she stayed.
첫째 날: "이 마늘 정말 끔찍해!"

열째 날: "햇빛이 그리워!"

스무째 날: "못 참겠어!"

하지만 곰이는 남았어요.
14 — 15

Spread 9 — Just One More Day

💭 🍚
Day thirty. Day fifty. Day seventy.

Gom-i sang little songs in the dark.
She counted her heartbeats.
She thought about warm rice
and all the things she wanted so badly.

"Just one more day.
Just one more day."
서른째 날. 쉰째 날. 일흔째 날.

어둠 속에서 작은 노래를 불렀어요.

"하루만 더. 하루만 더."
16 — 17

Spread 10 — Day One Hundred

✨ 🌅
On the hundredth morning,
something happened.

Gom-i looked down at her paws —
but they weren't paws anymore.
They were hands.
Small, human hands.

She laughed. She cried.
She spun around under the blue, blue sky.

She was human.
백 번째 아침, 무언가 일어났어요.

손이었어요. 작고 사람의 손.

사람이 된 거예요.
18 — 19

Spread 11 — A New Beginning

🌅 👶
They married under the sacred tree
on Baekdu Mountain,
and they had a son.
They named him Dangun.

Dangun grew up to build
the very first kingdom of Korea.
He called it Joseon —
the Land of Morning Calm.

And it all began
with a bear who wouldn't give up.
이름은 단군이었어요.

조선 — 고요한 아침의 나라.

포기하지 않은 곰 한 마리에서
시작되었어요.

Spread 12 — Back Page

🌙 ✨
And that is how Korea began.

But what about Ho-rang?

Don't worry — he's still out there,
running through the mountains.

And every time Gom-i tells this story,
he sits right next to her and says:

"Tell them about the garlic!
Tell them how TERRIBLE it was!"

The End.
이렇게 한국이 시작되었어요.

"마늘 이야기 해줘!
얼마나 끔찍했는지!"

끝.
